1. India's Relationship with Gulf Countries India has had a complex and multifaceted relationship with Gulf countries. While there has been significant economic cooperation, particularly in the areas of energy and trade, there have also been diplomatic tensions. The Gulf countries are home to a large expatriate Indian community, whose welfare is a critical concern for India. Issues like labor rights and diplomatic incidents can strain these relationships.

2. Citizens Giving Up Citizenship There has been a noticeable trend of Indians renouncing their citizenship to migrate to other countries. The reasons for this can include the search for better economic opportunities, higher quality of life, and political reasons. This phenomenon is reflective of both global migration trends and specific issues within India.

4. FTA (Free Trade Agreement) Failures India has faced challenges in negotiating Free Trade Agreements with various countries and economic blocs. These failures can be attributed to disagreements over terms, concerns about the impact on domestic industries, and protectionist policies. Successful FTAs can enhance economic growth, but failed negotiations can hinder international trade prospects.

5. Low Global Hunger Index India's position on the Global Hunger Index indicates significant issues with malnutrition, food security, and child stunting. Despite various government schemes and programs aimed at addressing these problems, challenges remain in terms of implementation, corruption, and reach.

6. Low Peace Index India's ranking on the Global Peace Index reflects internal and external challenges to maintaining peace. Issues like communal violence, insurgency in certain regions, and border conflicts contribute to this low ranking. Efforts to enhance internal security and diplomatic strategies are essential.
