India is facing a lot of serious issues that are worrying many of its citizens. The strong relationships with Gulf countries and Russia are now under strain, which affects India's trade and security. Many people are giving up their Indian citizenship because they feel unsure about the country's future.

The falling democracy index shows that people are worried about the weakening of democratic values. Failed Free Trade Agreements and a low global hunger index point to economic problems and food insecurity. India's low peace index and the high number of MPs with criminal records raise concerns about safety and political honesty.

The low human development index and ongoing human rights issues show that social policies need improvement. India's international relationships are also struggling, which affects its global reputation. The Prime Minister's support for controversial figures and divisive speech is worsening social divisions.

The decline in press freedom and the spread of false information are limiting free speech and trust in the media. High taxes without enough public services are causing frustration. Economic inequality is growing, with rich people getting special treatment and often avoiding justice through bribes, while the poor faces fake charges and criticism, making social divides worse.

As citizens see these problems unfold, there is a growing need for real reforms and honest governance to address these serious concerns and bring back hope for the country's future.
